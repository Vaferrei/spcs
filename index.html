2

We are all different. Some taller, some shorter, some better at sports, some better at sciences. Most people by now probably have already reached that conclusion. Another thing most of us agree on is that we should all be allowed and able to practice our religion or faith. So why is it so hard to combine these two together?

Christians should be concerned with this, because the Church, the Bible and Jesus taught us we should treat everyone well and everyone is important. Also if we put ourselves on a disabled person’s shoes we know it would be really bad if we couldn’t even practice our religion when it is supposed to be there for all and to help the ones that most need.

3

There are many ways a Christian might respond to this. Some of those are: by speaking with people in their parish to try to make their parish more inclusive towards disabled people, by explaining to other people how bad it is to not be included due to a disability and by talking and trying to persuade people to take measures to ensure inclusion towards disabled people.

4

This is another time when the Bible proves to be a really important source and a really useful “tool”. It teaches us that we are all important, that we shouldn’t be judged by how we look or how we are physically and that we should serve and help each other. Passages from the Bible are explained under if you want to read more.

"So God created humans to be like himself" - Genesis 1:27

This quote shows us that we are all very important and that there is no such thing as a worthless person. If we were created to be like God, which is perfect, why aren't we important, good (in the sense of being functional) and worth many things?

People judge others by what they look like, but I judge people by what is in their hearts" - 1 Samuel 16:7

This quote teaches us that we shouldn't judge others bases on how they are or might look physically. It teaches to rather judge people based on what they are like. On the context of disability this is teaching us not to judge or make preconceptions about someone based on the fact that they have a disability.

"And if your Lord and teacher has washed your feet, you should do the same for eachother" - John 13:13

This quote from the Bible is about Jesus washing the disciples' feet. If Jesus did this to the disciples then we should also do it to eachother. In other words we should all serve and help eachother.

"I command you not to make fun of the deaf or to cause a blind person to stumble." - Leviticus 19:14

This quote from the Bible teaches us that we shouldn't make fun or cause harm to those with disabilities.
Another passage from the Bible that teaches us we should help disabled people is the healing of a paralytic at Bethzatha by Jesus.
The parable of the Sheep and the Goats is yet another passage from the Bible that explains to us that we should treat each other as well as we would treat Jesus and that we should help other people.

Disabled people should be included in the Church. Here are some reasons why I think this:

  • Firstly, because the Church and the Bible teach us to treat everyone equally and help them. This is important, because part of treating everyone equally and helping them is ensuring their ability to do everything (or most things) other people would be able to do.
  • Secondly, because the Church and the Bible teach us we should practice our faith. This is important, because their faith and their relationship with God may be weakened if they aren’t able to practice their religion.
  • Lastly, because it is a basic right for people to be allowed and to be able to practice their religion. This is important because it is the law and everyone’s rights, and these should be followed/ensured, (respectively).

5

The Church is already tackling this problem very well. Some of the ways It does this are: by teaching people that they should do an effort to include people, by teaching priests how disabled people can perform the sacraments and by collecting money if needed to be spent on making the Church more inclusive towards disabled people.

Some difficulties people may face when trying to make the church more inclusive towards disabled people are: there might not be the appropriate services or tools needed, it can be expensive to buy those tools and it can be hard to ensure disabled people are included.
